More Info: Don Quixote

Author: Miguel de Cervantes

Published: 1605 (Part 1), 1615 (Part 2)

Synopsis: Follow the adventures of Alonso Quijano, a noble who reads so many chivalric romances that he loses his mind and believes himself to be a knight-errant named Don Quixote de la Mancha. With his loyal squire Sancho Panza, he sets out on a series of fantastical and often comical quests.

Themes: Idealism vs. reality, social satire, the power of imagination.

More Info: The Great Gatsby

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

Published: 1925

Synopsis: The story primarily concerns the wealthy Jay Gatsby and his pursuit of the former debutante Daisy Buchanan. Considered one of the greatest works of American literature, the novel explores themes of wealth, love, idealism, and the American Dream.

Themes: The American Dream, wealth and class, love and illusion, the past and the present.

More Info: Pride and Prejudice

Author: Jane Austen

Published: 1813

Synopsis: The novel follows the witty and independent Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates the complexities of love, social class, and family expectations in 19th-century England. Her initial prejudices against the proud Mr. Darcy gradually give way to a deeper understanding and affection.

Themes: Love and marriage, social class, prejudice and first impressions, independence and societal expectations.

More Info: 1984

Author: George Orwell

Published: 1949

Synopsis: Set in a dystopian future where a totalitarian regime known as "the Party" controls every aspect of people's lives, the novel follows Winston Smith as he rebels against the oppressive rule and seeks individual thought and freedom.

Themes: Totalitarianism, surveillance, thought control, language and power, loss of individuality.
